About This Tea:

Origin: The Kashmir Valley – known for its snow-capped mountains and rich food traditions 🏔️

Ingredients:

  • Green tea leaves (usually gunpowder tea)
  • Cold water
  • Baking soda (small pinch)
  • Salt (instead of sugar)
  • Milk (whole milk for creaminess)
  • Optional: crushed almonds, cardamom, or saffron

Flavor Profile:

  • Earthy
  • Salty
  • Creamy

Why You'll Love It 💖

  • Unique pink color and salty flavor
  • A cozy winter tradition in Kashmir
  • Great with bread, nuts, or local sweets

🌸 Make It at Home

Brewing Noon Chai takes a little patience — but the results are a warm, pink comfort in a cup.

  • 🔥 Boil 2 cups of water and add 2 tsp green tea leaves + a pinch of baking soda.
  • 🌀 Simmer for 10–15 minutes, stirring to deepen the color (it will turn reddish).
  • 💧 Add cold water to shock the tea, which helps turn it pink when milk is added.
  • 🥛 Add milk slowly while stirring, then salt to taste (no sugar!).
  • 🌸 Optionally: Garnish with crushed almonds, cardamom, or saffron.
  • ☕ Strain and enjoy your pink tea in a traditional cup.
  • Use a copper pot for authentic flavor and rich color.
  • Noon Chai isn’t sweet — embrace the saltiness and try it with bread!
  • ✨ Rehan’s Note
    Personally, I add at least 4–5 spoons of sugar to my cup before drinking it!😋
